Ada Lovelace

Born: 1815 | Died: 1852

Considered the first computer programmer for work on the Analytical Engine.

Major contributions

  • Notes on Analytical Engine
  • Early algorithm concepts

Biography

Early Life

Born in 1815 in London, Ada Lovelace was the daughter of poet Lord Byron and mathematician Anne Isabella Milbanke. Encouraged by her mother to pursue mathematics and logic, Ada showed an early fascination with numbers and mechanical inventions, which later shaped her visionary ideas about computation.

Scientific Work

Lovelace collaborated with Charles Babbage on his Analytical Engine, translating an article about the machine and adding extensive notes that contained what is recognized as the first computer algorithm. She uniquely foresaw that machines could go beyond arithmetic, suggesting they might one day compose music or create art.
