Galileo Galilei

Born: 1564 | Died: 1642

Astronomer and physicist; improvements to telescope and heliocentrism.

Major contributions

  • Improved telescope
  • Astronomical observations supporting heliocentrism

Biography

Early Life

Born in Pisa, Italy, in 1564, Galileo studied medicine before turning to mathematics and physics. His curiosity about motion and celestial bodies shaped the scientific revolution.

Scientific Work

He improved the telescope, discovered the moons of Jupiter, and supported the Copernican view that the Earth orbits the Sun. His studies of motion laid the groundwork for classical mechanics.
