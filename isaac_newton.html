Isaac Newton

Born: 1643 | Died: 1727

English mathematician, physicist; laws of motion & gravity.

Major contributions

  • Reflecting telescope
  • Calculus (independently developed)
  • Laws of motion & universal gravitation

Biography

Early Life

Born in 1643 in Woolsthorpe, England, Newton was an inquisitive child fascinated by mechanics and nature. He studied at Trinity College, Cambridge, where he developed groundbreaking ideas during the Great Plague years.

Scientific Work

Newton formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation, uniting celestial and terrestrial physics. He also developed calculus and conducted pioneering studies in optics.
