Leonardo da Vinci

Born: 1452 | Died: 1519

Renaissance polymath who designed early machines and studies of flight.

Major contributions

  • Flying machine sketches
  • Anatomical studies
  • Innovative mechanical designs

Biography

Early Life

Born in Vinci, Italy, in 1452, Leonardo was apprenticed to painter Andrea del Verrocchio. His insatiable curiosity spanned art, anatomy, engineering, and nature.

Scientific Work

Da Vinci documented detailed observations of human anatomy, flight mechanics, and hydraulics. His notebooks reveal designs for flying machines, tanks, and other innovations centuries ahead of his time.

Legacy

A symbol of the Renaissance, Leonardo blended art and science like no other. His genius continues to inspire inventors, artists, and scientists around the world.
