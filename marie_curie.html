Marie Curie

Born: 1867 | Died: 1934

Physicist/chemist; research on radioactivity, discovered radium & polonium.

Major contributions

  • Discovery of radium
  • Discovery of polonium
  • Portable X-ray work

Biography

Early Life

Born Maria Skłodowska in Warsaw, Poland, in 1867, Curie pursued higher education in Paris after facing barriers in her home country. She married fellow scientist Pierre Curie, forming one of science’s most famous partnerships.

Scientific Work

[Curie discovered the elements polonium and radium, and coined the term “radioactivity.” Her precise research techniques defined the study of radioactive substances.

Legacy

Curie discovered the elements polonium and radium, and coined the term “radioactivity.” Her precise research techniques defined the study of radioactive substances.
