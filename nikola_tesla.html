Nikola Tesla

Born: 1856 | Died: 1943

Serbian-American inventor; AC electricity and radio innovations.

Major contributions

  • AC induction motor
  • Tesla coil
  • Polyphase power systems

Biography

Early Life

Born in 1856 in Smiljan, Croatia, Tesla showed exceptional memory and imagination. After studying engineering, he emigrated to the United States in 1884 to work with Thomas Edison.

Scientific Work

Tesla developed alternating current (AC) power systems, the basis of modern electrical grids. He also pioneered radio, remote control, and early concepts of wireless power transmission.
