Biography

Early Life

Born in New York in 1918, Feynman displayed early brilliance in mathematics and curiosity about how things work. He earned his Ph.D. from Princeton, studying under John Archibald Wheeler.

Scientific Work

He revolutionized quantum electrodynamics, introducing Feynman diagrams that transformed particle physics. He also contributed to the Manhattan Project and developed influential teaching methods.
