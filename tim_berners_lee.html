Tim Berners-Lee

Born: 1955 | Died:

Inventor of the World Wide Web.

Major contributions

  • Invented the World Wide Web
  • HTTP & HTML concepts

Biography

Early Life

Born in London in 1955, Berners-Lee grew up with computer scientists as parents. His curiosity about information systems guided his studies at Oxford.

Scientific Work

At CERN, he invented the World Wide Web, creating HTML, HTTP, and the first web browser. His open-access vision made global information sharing possible.

Legacy

Knighted in 2004, Berners-Lee continues to champion privacy and open standards. His invention changed how humanity communicates and learns.
